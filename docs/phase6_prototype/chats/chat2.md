## User

Two reference photographs I'd like the mood board to anchor on:

1. Augusta National Hole 12 — the stone bridge across Rae's
   Creek, the green visible behind it, the pines and azaleas
   framing the composition. Golden-hour light, atmospheric,
   the stone bridge as sculptural object embedded in the
   landscape. This is the "every element belongs" principle
   the brief calls out in section 2.

2. Augusta National Hole 13 — the azaleas in full bloom,
   the green and fairway curving against them, the deep
   greens of the pines beyond. The colour palette here is
   the brief's primary anchor — Heritage Green register,
   with the azalea pink as an example of how a single
   accent can read as luxurious without being decorative.

Both images are widely reproduced from Masters coverage.
Claude Design should be able to source equivalents from
The Masters official photography or from premium course
photographers' (Brian Oar, LC Lambrecht) public portfolios
as part of mood board assembly.
